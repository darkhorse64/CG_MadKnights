The Game

This is an implementation of the board game Knight Isolation, a Udacity model game.

Click here for the referee.

  Rules

The game is played on a 8x8 chess board with two players. Each player has one single piece: a knight. The goal of the game is to "isolate" the opponent knight.

Whenever a knight occupies a square, that square becomes unavailable for the remainder of the game. An open square available for a knight to move into is called a "liberty". The first player with no remaining liberties for their piece loses the game, and their opponent is declared the winner.

The initial positions of the pieces are random but symmetric with respect to the board center.

How to play:

White starts the game. Each player alternate taking turns moving their knight to an available square. Knights can move to any open square that is 2-rows and 1-column or 2-columns and 1-row away from their current position on the board.

The game ends when one player has no available moves.

Ending a turn:
  • A bot must output the destination coordinates of its piece. Coordinates are expressed in chess notation e.g. e3
Wrong output:
  • If the destination square is outside of the board,
  • If the destination square is not available,
  • If the move is not a knight move,

the game will end and the other player will win.

  Expert Rules

  • The game has been deliberately designed to be simple. Focus on search algorithms !
    • Victory Conditions
    • Be the last to move.
    Loss Conditions
    • You cannot move your knight.
    • You do not respond in time.
    • You output an unrecognized command or an illegal move.

      Game Input/Output

    Initial input
    One line: color: the color of your unique piece (w: white, b: black).
    Input for one game turn
    First 8 lines: line: a string of characters representing one horizontal row of the grid, top to bottom. (.: empty, #: blocked, w: white, b: black).
    Next line: lastMove: the last move by the opponent (null if it's the first turn).

    Next line: moveCount: the number of legal moves for this turn.

    Next moveCount lines: move: a string of characters representing a legal move.
    Output
    A single line containing the coordinates where you want to move to, e.g. e3.
    • You can display a (short) comment in the viewer by adding a space after the move and writing its content, e.g. e3 message.
    • You can play a random move (chosen amongst all legal moves) by outputting random instead of specific coordinates.

    Constraints
    Response time first turn is ≤ 1000 ms.
    Response time per turn is ≤ 100 ms.
